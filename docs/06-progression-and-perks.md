# 06 — Progression, Perks & Player Advantage

## Perks are verbs, not percentages
**Avoid flat percentage perks.** Roll the buff into a distinct, engaged action.

> Anybody can pick up a chaingun and pull the trigger. Specialists do controlled bursts,
> hose an area, bounce rounds off a wall.

A perk that lets you **spin up a chaingun for free** *is* a flat buff — you'll always do it —
but it reads as a technique and it changes how you play the turn. That's the bar. If a perk
can be described as "+10% damage," rewrite it until it's a verb.

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| +15% chaingun damage | **Controlled Burst** — split a burst across two targets |
| +10% accuracy | **Spin Up** — free, and shrinks ring 1 (see `02`) |
| +20% armor | **Angle It** — reaction: turn your thickest plate toward a shot |

Perk slots are capped by **link tier** (see `04`) — a low-tier link carries few perks, but
the player picks which, and may choose the highest-tier ones.

## Player advantage — deliberate asymmetry
Some advantage is unprogrammed: a human prioritizes targets better than an AI that just
shoots whoever is statistically scariest. The rest is **designed in**, and is not a bug:

**1. Players combine; enemies are single-purpose.**
An opposition unit exists to do one thing — a tesla bot charges allies and shocks whoever's
close; a chaingun mech brings two chainguns. A player cyborg with the right build does **both
at once**.

**2. Players get unfair verbs. Enemies never do.**
| Verb | Player | Enemy |
|---|---|---|
| Knock prone | yes | no |
| Instakill weak enemies | yes | no |
| Rip the matrix out of a damaged unit | yes | **never** |

This is a hard rule. Nothing in that column ever happens *to* the player.

**3. High-impact weapons favour the player.**
An enemy chaingun is scary because volume of fire is coming your way. A *player* chaingun
combos: alternate attacks, pair it with a booster, and a matrix with dual-weapon capability
runs **dual chainguns on a chassis never designed for it.**

## Progression targets
The visual arc matters. Players start as scavengers and should *look* like it.

> **Jerry, run 1:** shotgun, metal saw for an offhand, empty barrel on his back. He can cut
> scrap, shoot anyone who gets close, and get out with loot.
>
> **Jerry, run 20:** autogun arm, big metal shield offhand, and a mulebot trailing him
> hauling loot — he doesn't even bother cutting it up until he's back on the ship.

Same character, same verbs, unrecognizable silhouette. Progression should read at a glance
before any number is checked.
